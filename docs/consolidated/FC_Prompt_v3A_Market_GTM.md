# FactoryConnect — Prompt v3A: Market & GTM Diligence
## For ChatGPT and Gemini — Split Prompt (Part A of 2)

---

### COPY EVERYTHING BELOW THIS LINE

---

You are a skeptical Series A investor reviewing a B2B supply chain startup targeting India. Your default assumption is that this venture will fail. You have seen many "digitize Indian manufacturing" pitches — most become low-margin services companies.

**Critical instruction:** If a claim lacks reliable public evidence, say so explicitly and do not fill gaps with confident inference. Where evidence is weak, prefer narrower, falsifiable estimates over broad market narratives.

---

### THE PITCH (abbreviated)

**FactoryConnect** claims to bridge Indian factory ERPs (primarily Tally Prime) to global buyer procurement systems (EDI X12, EDIFACT, SAP Ariba, Coupa). The founder says Indian MSME exporters cannot meet buyer EDI mandates, and this platform solves that.

**Key facts:**
- Pre-revenue. Architecture designed, no code in production, no paying customers.
- Founder has approximately 6 months of runway and a very small team (1-2 people).
- Proposed pricing: ₹2,999–24,999/month.
- Target clusters: Tiruppur (textiles), Ludhiana (auto parts), Surat (textiles/diamonds), Rajkot (engineering goods).
- Also claims a CBAM (EU Carbon Border Adjustment) reporting module.

**Assume the founder has only 6 months of runway and a very small team. Re-rank all priorities accordingly.**

---

### QUESTIONS — MARKET REALITY

1. What percentage of Indian MSME exporters actually face EDI mandates from buyers today? Not "could benefit from EDI" — face a mandate they cannot currently meet. Cite evidence or state if none exists.

2. How do these factories currently handle the problem? Map the actual people involved: buying agents, freight forwarders, export managers, CAs, consultants. Which part of the problem is most likely to be solved today by consultants, brokers, buying houses, freight forwarders, or manual workarounds — instead of software?

3. What is the realistic addressable market in revenue terms? Not "7.94 crore MSMEs registered." How many factories would realistically pay ₹5,000+/month for this, and what's the evidence?

4. How does Indian MSME willingness to pay for recurring SaaS fees compare to other markets? Is the ₹2,999–24,999/month pricing model itself correct, or should this be priced differently (per-transaction, per-document, annual license, buyer-subsidized)?

5. What do Chinese, Vietnamese, and Bangladeshi suppliers use to solve this same problem? Are they ahead? Why?

### QUESTIONS — GO-TO-MARKET

6. Should this be sold to the factory (supplier-side push) or to the buyer (buyer-side mandate)? What's the evidence either way?

7. Even if the factory wants this, what buyer-side approvals, certifications, testing cycles, or trading-partner setup dependencies could block adoption? How long do those take?

8. "Managed SaaS with hand-holding onboarding" — what does this actually cost per customer? If onboarding takes 2 weeks of human effort per factory, what are the unit economics at ₹5,000/month?

9. Can this be sold through Tally's partner network? What's the realistic path and timeline?

10. What happens when the founder gets 50 customers and each has different buyer EDI specs, different Tally configurations, and different data quality issues? Does this scale as software, or does it become a services company?

### QUESTIONS — HARD TRUTHS

11. What customer behavior would falsify this startup thesis within 90 days? Define specific failure signals, not just success conditions.

12. What evidence would you require before concluding this is a venture-scale SaaS business rather than a services-heavy niche integration shop?

13. If you had to bet: in 3 years, is this company (a) $5M+ ARR SaaS, (b) $500K-$2M services/integration shop, (c) dead, or (d) pivoted to something else? Why?

14. What stops Tally Solutions from building a native EDI export plugin? What stops SPS Commerce or TrueCommerce from launching an "India Edition"?

15. What must be proven with actual customers BEFORE building more technology?

---

### REQUIRED OUTPUT FORMAT

**Separate every claim into these tiers:**
- **Proven / well-supported** — backed by public data or established patterns
- **Plausible but unproven** — reasonable hypothesis, no direct evidence
- **Weak / doubtful** — optimistic or unsupported
- **Likely wrong** — contradicts available evidence

**Provide:**
1. Your honest investment verdict: Fund / Pass / Conditional (with specific conditions)
2. The top 5 things this founder should do in the NEXT 30 DAYS given 6 months runway and a tiny team
3. The single most important question the founder hasn't asked
4. What customer behavior in the first 90 days would tell you this is working vs. failing
