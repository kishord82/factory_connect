01 — Executive Summary

FactoryConnect occupies a unique position at the intersection of three converging forces: India's $116.7B engineering exports sector needing digital procurement connectivity, the EU's CBAM definitive phase (Jan 2026) creating mandatory carbon reporting obligations, and Indian SME factories still running on Tally/Zoho with zero EDI capability. No existing solution bridges all three.

This strategy synthesizes two independent AI-generated GTM frameworks — a "Cluster + Channel + Trigger" model and a "CBAM Compliance Wedge" model — with primary research on Indian export institutions, cluster economics, and regulatory timelines. The result is a five-motion GTM engine that sequences motions by urgency: CBAM compliance creates the door-opener, Tally partner embedding delivers the distribution, buyer mandates create pull, cluster blitzes create local density, and digital inbound scales the funnel.

Core Thesis: FactoryConnect is not a self-serve SaaS product. It is a "productized service with SaaS inside" — a managed integration platform that hand-holds Indian SME factories from paper/Tally workflows into digital procurement compliance. The GTM must reflect this reality.

02 — Market Landscape & Research Findings

EU CBAM: The Compliance Catalyst

The EU's Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism entered its definitive phase on January 1, 2026. This is the single most important market catalyst for FactoryConnect. Key facts from research:

What changed: Reporting shifted from quarterly (transitional) to annual declarations. Reported emissions must now be independently verified by third parties. EU importers must purchase and surrender CBAM certificates — at current EU ETS prices, approximately €173.8/tonne for steel exports.

Sectors covered: Cement, iron & steel, aluminium, fertilisers, electricity, hydrogen. Iron and steel + aluminium represent 27% of India's CBAM-covered exports to the EU.

India impact: EEPC India projects a 10% decline in engineering exports to Europe if CBAM issues are unresolved. World Bank estimates export losses of $771M. Indian steel MSMEs are especially vulnerable — they lack the systems to measure, report, or reduce embedded carbon.

Omnibus simplifications (Oct 2025): The EU adopted simplifications to reduce administrative burden, but core obligations remain. This actually helps FactoryConnect — simplified rules make a standardized SaaS approach more viable than custom consulting.

30,000+ direct members. Apex body of Indian exporters since 1965. Runs 100+ international MoUs. Hosts trade fairs, training programmes, and the Indian Trade Portal. Key events: India Fashion & Lifestyle Show (Tokyo), CHWE Spring (Shenzhen), Thaifex-Anuga (Bangkok) in 2026. Partnership angle: Co-branded CBAM compliance workshops at FIEO events. Offer FactoryConnect as a "recommended digital trade tool" via the Indian Trade Portal.

12,000+ members, 60% SMEs. Sponsored by Ministry of Commerce. Focuses specifically on engineering exports — the sector most exposed to CBAM. Membership is digital via DGFT portal. Partnership angle: EEPC is actively concerned about CBAM's 10% export decline projection. Position FactoryConnect as their technology solution partner for CBAM readiness.

New e-Platform connecting 6L+ IEC holders with Indian Missions, Export Promotion Councils, and trade experts. TallyPrime supports two-way data transfer via integration framework. Partnership angle: Become a certified Tally integration partner. Build a TallyPrime extension that syncs invoice/PO data to FactoryConnect. Distribute through Tally's existing partner network of 5-Star certified partners.

04 — Five GTM Motions

The unified strategy sequences five complementary motions. Each motion has a distinct trigger, channel, and conversion path. They compound: CBAM opens the door, Tally distributes, buyers pull, clusters densify, and digital scales.

01
Highest Priority

CBAM Compliance Pull

Trigger: EU CBAM definitive phase is live. Indian steel/aluminium/cement exporters face mandatory third-party verified carbon reporting and certificate costs of ~€174/tonne.

Message: "Your EU buyer now pays a carbon tax on your shipments. If you can't report your embedded emissions, they'll switch to a supplier who can."

Channel: EEPC India, FIEO, industry association workshops, CA/tax consultant referrals.

Conversion: Free CBAM readiness assessment → 30-day shadow mode → paid subscription.

02
Highest Priority

Tally Partner Embed

Trigger: 11 lakh businesses use Tally. Every factory that exports eventually needs to generate EDI-compliant documents. Tally can't do this natively.

Message: "FactoryConnect turns your Tally into an EDI-ready export platform. No migration, no new software to learn."

Channel: Tally 5-Star Certified Partners, TallyPrime Extensions marketplace, Tally partner events.

Conversion: Tally partner demo → co-sold installation → managed onboarding.

03
Medium Priority

Buyer Mandate Push

Trigger: When a global buyer (Walmart, Bosch, H&M) mandates digital POs/ASNs from Indian suppliers, those factories have no choice. This is the most powerful conversion trigger.

Message: "Your buyer requires EDI 850/856/810. FactoryConnect gets you compliant in 2 weeks, not 6 months."

Channel: Direct outreach to procurement heads at 2-3 anchor buyers. Offer FactoryConnect as "supplier enablement" at zero cost to the buyer.

Conversion: Buyer partnership → mandate letter to suppliers → FactoryConnect as the recommended platform.

04
Medium Priority

Cluster Blitz

Trigger: Export clusters are tight-knit communities. One factory's success spreads by word of mouth at chai stalls and association meetings.

Message: Cluster-specific, local language. "Rajkot's engineering exporters are losing EU orders to CBAM non-compliance. 12 factories in your industrial estate are already on FactoryConnect."

Channel: Local industry association events, cluster-level workshops, on-ground sales team (1-2 people per cluster).

Conversion: Workshop → 5-factory cohort pilot → case study → cluster-wide rollout.

05
Scale Phase

Digital Inbound

Trigger: Factory owners/managers searching for "CBAM compliance for Indian exporters", "Tally EDI integration", "Ariba supplier onboarding India".

Message: Content-led — blog posts, webinars, CBAM calculators, Tally integration guides.

Channel: SEO, Google Ads (high-intent keywords), LinkedIn (targeting export managers), YouTube (Tally integration tutorials in Hindi/regional languages).

Conversion: Content → CBAM assessment tool → demo request → managed onboarding.

Buyer-Side Partnerships (Demand Pull)

The most powerful channel is convincing 2-3 anchor buyers to mandate FactoryConnect. Target profile: large EU/US buyer sourcing from Indian SME clusters, already using Ariba/Coupa, and concerned about CBAM reporting from their supply chain.

Target Buyers (illustrative): A European auto OEM sourcing forged parts from Ludhiana. An EU fashion retailer sourcing knitwear from Tiruppur. A US industrial distributor buying engineering goods from Rajkot. In each case, the buyer benefits from: automated PO/invoice flow (reduces their procurement cost), CBAM-ready supply chain data, and supplier reliability scores.

Pitch to buyer: "We'll onboard your top 20 Indian suppliers at no cost to you. You get EDI-compliant, CBAM-ready supply chain data. We monetize through supplier subscriptions."

Pricing Rationale

₹2,999 Starter is positioned at the "no-brainer" threshold for any exporter facing CBAM obligations. It's less than what they'd pay a CA for a single CBAM consultation. The CBAM-only tier is the wedge — once they see the value, upgrading to Standard (which adds the EDI bridge) is the natural next step when a buyer mandate arrives.

₹9,999 Standard is the core revenue driver. At ~$120/month, it's 1/10th the cost of US-based EDI solutions (SPS Commerce charges ~$300-500/user/month). The managed onboarding inclusion is critical — Indian SMEs won't self-serve.

₹24,999 Premium targets exporters doing ₹50Cr+ in EU trade, where CBAM certificate costs alone could be ₹10-20L/year. At ₹3L/year, FactoryConnect is trivial compared to the compliance cost they're already paying.

Pilot Pricing (Days 1-60): Offer the first 20 factories in each P0 cluster 3 months free on Standard tier in exchange for case study rights and feedback. This creates the "12 factories in your industrial estate" social proof needed for cluster blitz.

07 — Managed SaaS Playbook

Indian SME factories are not self-serve SaaS buyers. The typical factory owner is 45-60, runs Tally on a desktop, communicates via WhatsApp, and makes decisions based on trust (their CA's recommendation, their association president's endorsement). The "managed SaaS" model must account for this reality.

The Shadow Mode Trial

This is the single most important onboarding innovation. Instead of asking factories to switch systems, FactoryConnect runs in shadow mode — parallel to their existing process — for 30 days.

How it works: FC connects to Tally via the integration framework (read-only). For 30 days, FC mirrors every invoice and PO, generating the EDI equivalent alongside. At the end of 30 days, the factory owner sees a side-by-side: "Here's what your buyer received via email/fax. Here's what FactoryConnect would have sent via EDI. Same data, zero manual entry, CBAM-compliant." Zero risk. No process change. No disruption.

Key Managed SaaS Principles

WhatsApp-first support: 80% of Indian SME communication happens on WhatsApp. FC's support, onboarding updates, and even CBAM reports should be delivered via WhatsApp Business API. No email-heavy workflows.

Regional language: Onboarding materials and support in Hindi, Tamil (Tiruppur), Punjabi (Ludhiana), and Gujarati (Surat/Rajkot).

On-site first 5: For the first 5 customers in each cluster, send an onboarding engineer on-site. This builds trust and creates local references who can vouch for FC at association meetings.

CA as co-pilot: Train the factory's CA to use FC's CBAM dashboard. The CA becomes the ongoing champion because it makes their compliance work easier.

10 — Regulatory & Compliance Landscape

EU CBAM (Definitive Phase)

Status: Live since January 1, 2026. Annual declarations required. Third-party verification mandatory. CBAM certificates must be purchased by EU importers.

Covered sectors: Cement, iron & steel, aluminium, fertilisers, electricity, hydrogen.

Cost to exporters: ~€173.8/tonne for steel. India's CBAM-exposed exports to EU valued at $8.2B.

Omnibus simplification (Oct 2025): Reduced admin burden but core obligations remain. Helps FactoryConnect — standardized reporting is easier to productize than custom consulting.

FC opportunity: Every Indian exporter to the EU in covered sectors now needs emissions data, third-party verification support, and annual reporting. This is FC's primary wedge.

India DPDP Act / Rules 2025

Status: Rules notified November 13, 2025. Full compliance mandatory by May 13, 2027 (18-month window).

Key requirements for FactoryConnect:

Consent management: FC must collect verifiable consent for processing factory/exporter personal data (contact details, financial data).

Breach notification: 72-hour notification to Data Protection Board and affected individuals.

Data retention: Automated deletion of personal data when purpose is fulfilled.

Cross-border transfer: FC stores data in India but processes some data via EU buyer systems — need to comply with transfer provisions.

Security safeguards: Technical and organizational measures appropriate to the risk.

Penalties: Up to ₹250 crore per violation.

FC action: Build DPDP compliance into the platform from Day 1. This is also a competitive moat — being DPDP-compliant is a trust signal for Indian SME customers.

11 — Risk Mitigation Matrix

High

Slow SME adoption / trust deficit

Indian factory owners are risk-averse. New software = suspicion.

Mitigation: Shadow mode trial (zero risk). CA endorsement. On-site first 5. Association-backed workshops. Local language. WhatsApp-first communication.

High

High onboarding cost erodes unit economics

Managed SaaS = human-heavy onboarding. At ₹9,999/month, CAC could exceed LTV.

Mitigation: Cohort-based onboarding (5 factories per batch). Tally partner co-sell reduces FC's direct sales cost. Pilot customers subsidized by case study value. Target 18-month LTV:CAC of 3:1 by Month 12.

Medium

CBAM regulatory changes / delays

EU could further simplify CBAM or delay enforcement. India-EU FTA negotiations could alter CBAM treatment.

Mitigation: CBAM is the wedge, not the entire product. EDI bridge + procurement compliance has value independent of CBAM. Diversify messaging: CBAM for urgency, EDI for ongoing value.

Medium

Tally partnership dependency

If Tally builds native EDI/CBAM features, FC loses its distribution advantage.

Mitigation: Multi-ERP strategy (Tally + Zoho + SAP B1). FC's value is the buyer-side connectivity (Ariba/Coupa/EDI), not just Tally integration. Build switching costs through buyer relationships.

Medium

Buyer-side partnership takes too long

Enterprise buyers have long procurement cycles. Getting a buyer to mandate FC could take 6-12 months.

Mitigation: Don't wait for buyer mandates. CBAM compliance + Tally integration create supplier-side demand. Buyer mandates are an accelerant, not a prerequisite. Start buyer outreach on Day 1 but don't gate revenue on it.

Low

Competitor entry from EDI incumbents

SPS Commerce or TrueCommerce could build India-specific features.

Mitigation: FC's moat is local: Tally integration, on-ground presence, regional language support, CA network, cluster-level density. US companies won't invest in this for a ₹10K/month price point. Move fast — first 100 factories in each cluster creates a network effect.

Medium

DPDP compliance complexity

Handling cross-border data (India → EU buyer systems) under DPDP + GDPR simultaneously.

Mitigation: Engage a data privacy consultant during Days 1-30. Implement privacy-by-design. Use DPDP compliance as a marketing advantage — "your data stays in India, DPDP-certified."

Low

Price sensitivity kills expansion revenue

Factories stay on ₹2,999 Starter and never upgrade.

Mitigation: Starter tier is intentionally CBAM-only. When a buyer mandate arrives (inevitable for active EU exporters), they must upgrade to Standard for EDI capabilities. The product architecture forces the upgrade.
