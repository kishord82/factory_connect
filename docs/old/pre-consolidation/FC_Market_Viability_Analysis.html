10. Recommended Action Plan

The Strategy: "CBAM Beachhead → Platform Expansion"

Don't try to build the entire FactoryConnect platform before going to market. Build the CBAM compliance module FIRST as a standalone product. Get paying customers. Use revenue and customer relationships to expand into the full platform. This de-risks everything.

Phase 0: Validate Before Building (Now → Month 1)

  1. Talk to 10 steel exporters. Cold message on LinkedIn/WhatsApp. Ask: "How are you handling CBAM reporting? What are you paying? Would you pay Rs 5,000/month for automated reporting?" If 7+ say yes → proceed. If 3 or fewer → reconsider.
  2. Talk to 5 CBAM consultants. Ask: "What do your clients struggle with most? What would an automated tool need to do?" These people understand the pain better than any market report.
  3. Create a landing page with pricing and a waitlist. Run Rs 10,000 of Google Ads targeting "CBAM compliance India." Measure signups.

Cost: Rs 15,000 + 2 weeks of time. If this doesn't validate, you've saved 12+ months.

Phase 1: CBAM MVP (Month 1-4)

  1. Build ONLY the CBAM carbon reporting module: data collection from Tally/Excel, emission calculation engine, EU quarterly report generation.
  2. Skip EDI, skip SAP Ariba, skip multi-buyer connectivity. CBAM only.
  3. Target: 5 paying beta customers by Month 4.
  4. Price: Rs 5,000/month (CBAM standalone) or Rs 15,000/month (CBAM + basic ERP sync).

Phase 2: First Revenue + Platform Foundation (Month 5-8)

  1. Onboard 20+ CBAM customers. Upsell to ERP integration where demand exists.
  2. Build Tally Prime connector (JSON-first, XML fallback for pre-7.0).
  3. Add basic order-to-invoice automation for top 3 customer requests.
  4. Start outreach to pharma exporters (Hyderabad cluster) and auto component makers (Pune cluster).

Phase 3: Full Platform + Scale (Month 9-18)

  1. Add EDI X12 support (Python Bots sidecar). First buyer connection live.
  2. Hire support person (Month 8-10). Hire sales rep for Pune or Chennai (Month 10-12).
  3. Approach buyer-side partnerships (Bosch India, pharma distributors).
  4. Target: 50+ customers, Rs 10 lakh+ MRR, approaching break-even.

11. Final Honest Assessment

Should You Build FactoryConnect?

YES, because:

  1. The market is real, large, and growing. India's EDI software market ($142M → $380M by 2035), B2B e-commerce ($200B by 2030), and manufacturing export growth ($254B+ FY26) all confirm structural demand.
  2. The problem is severe and compliance-driven. CBAM is mandatory EU law. EDI compliance is buyer-mandated. Factories lose POs and market access without solutions. This is a painkiller, not a vitamin.
  3. No one is serving this exact niche. SPS Commerce ($751M revenue) has zero India presence. TrueCommerce, Cleo — none of them serve Indian SMEs. The gap is real.
  4. Unit economics are excellent. 92%+ gross margins, LTV:CAC > 9x, CAC payback < 3 months. If you can sell, you can be profitable.
  5. You have founder-market fit. 19 years of integration experience, understanding of both India and USA business, technical ability to build the product yourself.
  6. Regulatory tailwinds are increasing. CBAM expands in 2028-2030. India's GST digitization continues. DPDP Act adds compliance needs. Every new regulation is a growth driver for FC.

BUT, with these critical conditions:

  1. Validate BEFORE building. Talk to 10 factory owners first. Don't spend 6 months building a product nobody wants to pay for. Rs 15,000 and 2 weeks of customer discovery saves you from the #1 B2B SaaS failure mode: building without validation.
  2. Ship CBAM first, not the full platform. The #2 failure mode for ambitious founders: trying to build everything at once. CBAM module is a standalone product that generates revenue in 3-4 months. The full platform can come later.
  3. Maintain personal income during Year 1. Whether it's the USA job (remote), consulting, or savings — do NOT depend on FC revenue for personal expenses until Month 14-18. The 11,223 Indian startup closures in 2025 were mostly runway-related.
  4. Move to India on schedule. This is NOT a product you can sell remotely from the USA. Indian manufacturing SME sales require in-person visits, chai at the factory, WhatsApp groups. Your May-June 2026 India move is essential.
  5. Set a hard Month 12 decision point. If you have fewer than 15 paying customers by Month 12, you need to honestly assess: is this a timing problem (solvable with more runway), a product problem (solvable with iteration), or a market problem (not solvable)?
